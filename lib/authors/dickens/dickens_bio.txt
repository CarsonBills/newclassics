Charles Dickens was an English novelist born on Feburary 7, 1812 and died June 9, 1870. He wrote over a dozen major novels in a style that has come to define Victorian Literature.  He repeated wrote about the plight of the lower classes and criticized the ugly truthes of Victorian Society. 

His writing style is highly sentimental and cinematic, focusing not only on the descriptions of events, but also of appearances.  Notably, Dickens carefully crafted his characters' memorable names to more accurately reflect their nature.
