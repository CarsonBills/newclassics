Mark Twain was an American writer born November 30, 1835 and died April 21, 1910. He wrote over a dozen novels over his lifetime.  In a time when literary pursuits were limited to university educated, Twain brought a distinctly American perspective to literature which turned away from the earlier traditions of ornate language.
<br>
<br>
Twain wrote in the actual language spoken by people of the day.  He favored this kind of clarity and humor over the over-wrought traditions of English Literature. Twain was also known for spoeaking directly to his readers within his works, commenting on the actions and characters for comic effect.
