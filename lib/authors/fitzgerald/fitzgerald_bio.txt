Author Biography
<br>
F. Scott Fitzgerald was an American novelist born September 24, 1896 and died December 21, 1940.  He finished four novels during his lifetime and many short stories.  His works exemplify the writings of the "Jazz Age". As a part of the "Lost Generation," his writings often reflected on a culture of material abundance, as well as the social and moral bankruptcy of the period.
<br>
<br>
Defined by ornate sentences, Fitzgerald uses common language to construct sentences that waft through ideas, people, or locations, often ending in unexpected metaphor.
